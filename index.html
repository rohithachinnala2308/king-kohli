Early Life

  • Kohli was born on November 5, 1988 in Delhi, India. He grew up in Delhi and was one of the first to train at the West Delhi Cricket Academy.
  • Virat Kohli is widely regarded as one of the greatest batsmen in the history of cricket and the greatest batsman of the modern era.
          He is an Indian international cricketer who has played for the Indian national cricket team in all three formats of the game - Test cricket,One-Day Internationals (ODIs), and Twenty20 Internationals (T20Is).
  • Kohli is a right-handed batsman and an occasional right-arm medium-pace bowler.
  • He has captained the Indian national team in the past and is considered one of India's most successful captains.
  • He captained the Indian under-19 cricket team to victory at the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup in Malaysia.
  • Kohli made his international debut for India in an ODI against Sri Lanka in 2008.

    International Career

  • Kohli has scored over 20,000 runs in international cricket, making him one of the highest run-scorers of all time.
  • He holds the record for the most centuries scored in ODI cricket with 50 centuries.
  • Kohli has been ranked as the number one batsman in all three international formats - Tests, ODIs, and T20Is.
  • He was a key member of the Indian teams that won the 2011 Cricket World Cup, 2013 Champions Trophy, and 2024 T20 World Cup.
  • Kohli captained India to three consecutive ICC Test mace wins in 2017, 2018, and 2019.

    • IPL Career

  • Kohli has played for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) franchise throughout his IPL career, making him the only player to have played for just one franchise in IPL history.
  • He is the highest run-scorer in IPL history with over 8,000 runs

    • Most Memorable Matches

    2014 T20 World Cup Semi-Final vs South Africa:

  • Kohli scored an unbeaten 72 off 44 balls to guide India to a 6-wicket win over South Africa while chasing 173. His knock included 5 fours and 2 sixes and helped India complete the chase in the final over

    • 2016 T20 World Cup Semi-Final vs West Indies:

  • After scoring 89 off 47 balls to help India post 192/2, Kohli dismissed set batsman Johnson Charles with his first ball of the match. Though India lost the match, Kohli's all-round performance was a highlight

    • 2019 T20I vs West Indies in Hyderabad:

  • Kohli smashed an unbeaten 94 off 50 balls, his highest T20I score at the time, to help India chase down West Indies' 207/5 with 8 balls to spare. He hit 6 fours and 6 sixes in his knock

    • 2019 T20I vs West Indies in Mumbai:

  • Kohli scored an unbeaten 70 off just 29 balls with 4 fours and 7 sixes at a strike rate of 241 to power India to 240/3. India won the match by 67 runs as West Indies finished on 173/8

    • 2024 T20 World Cup Final vs South Africa:

  • In his final T20I innings, Kohli scored 76 off 59 balls to anchor India's chase of 173. His knock helped India win by 7 runs and he was named Player of the Match. He announced his retirement from T20Is after the game.Kohli's ability to deliver match-winning performances in high-pressure knockout matches, especially in run chases, has been a hallmark of his T20I career. His calm demeanor and ability to pace his innings have made him one of the greatest T20I batters of all time.
